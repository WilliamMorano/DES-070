I jolted awake suddenly, as melancholy thoughts filled my head. Today was the day. The day of the final judgment. I prepared for today like any other day: showering, eating breakfast, brushing my teeth... However, a constant feeling of dread hovered over me, as I realized that the completion of each trivial morning chore was a step closer to the event. Eventually, the list was completed and I headed on my way to school.

Friends, teachers, cafeteria staff, and even the friendly bus drivers possessed no remedy to my saddened state. I simply could not shake the melancholy thoughts that arrived at the beginning of the day from my brain. I began to ponder how one could justify this dreaded, future event. Despite applying the majority of my brain power to this question, I could not come up with an answer, for this question dealt with the great existential questions of humankind…

What would life be like without suffering? Is suffering a necessary part of human existence? Is happiness a right or a privilege? What is more important: the ends or the means? Is it immoral for a vegetarian to eat animal crackers?

My mind was kept busy with these questions. However, when the final bell before the event screeched, my thoughts cleared.
